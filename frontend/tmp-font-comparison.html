Design System / Typography

Three serif pairings with Geist.
Choose the editorial voice for our product.

Option A

Instrument Serif

Warm, contemporary, quietly confident

Geist handles the interface. Instrument Serif steps in for moments of emphasis — page titles, data callouts, section headers. The contrast is subtle, not dramatic. It reads like a well-edited publication, not a fashion magazine.

Option B

DM Serif Display

Bold, authoritative, masthead energy

Heavier than Instrument — this pairing puts the serif front and center. It commands attention. Works well if we want headings to feel more like a newspaper headline than an editorial whisper. Stronger personality, less subtlety.

Option C

Lora

Approachable, versatile, quietly elegant

The most understated option. Lora doesn't call attention to itself — it just makes text feel considered. The serif presence is lighter, almost a texture. Best if we want typography that supports the content without becoming a character itself.

Side by Side

Which voice fits the product?

Recommended

Instrument Serif

+ Geist Sans

Warm and refined without being heavy. The italic is beautiful for emphasis moments. Closest to Harvey's editorial restraint — speaks quietly but with authority.

DM Serif Display

+ Geist Sans

Bolder presence. Commands the page. Works if we want headings to really anchor sections — but can feel heavy in a data-dense interface with many cards.

Lora

+ Geist Sans

Most subtle option. Almost disappears into the UI. Great versatility (works at body size too) but less distinctive — won't give us that editorial "moment."
